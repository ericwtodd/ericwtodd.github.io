I am a second-year PhD student at Northeastern University advised by [David Bau](https://baulab.info/). 

I am interested in understanding the internal representations of large neural networks, and how these machine-learned systems leverage their representations to perform different tasks.
My research interests generally include machine learning, applications in computer vision, and natural language processing (NLP) - with an emphasis on interpretability.

Prior to beginning my PhD, I studied Applied and Computational Mathematics (BS) and Computer Science (MS) at Brigham Young University (BYU).

# News
[October 2023] -- We released a new preprint! -- <br>
[Function Vectors in Large Language Models](https://functions.baulab.info/). We find that during in-context learning (ICL) language models create internal abstractions of general-purpose functions that are used to trigger a specific functional behavior.  We show how to extract a function vector and demonstrate that it can invoke the demonstrated behavior in a variety of contexts. We also investigate what a function vector contains and explore vector algebra for composing function vectors.
